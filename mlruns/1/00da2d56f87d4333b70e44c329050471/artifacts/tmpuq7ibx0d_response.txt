### Business Description

NVIDIA pioneered accelerated computing and is now a full-stack computing infrastructure company with data-center-scale offerings. The company designs GPUs, CPUs, DPUs, networking equipment, and comprehensive software platforms. Its CUDA programming model, introduced in 2006, created a massive developer ecosystem that underpins its competitive moat. NVIDIA's platforms serve AI training and inference, data analytics, scientific computing, 3D graphics, autonomous vehicles, and robotics [10-K FY2025](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1045810/000104581025000023/nvda-20250126.htm).

### Reporting Segments

NVIDIA reports through two operating segments [10-K FY2025](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1045810/000104581025000023/nvda-20250126.htm):

1. **Compute & Networking** -- Data Center accelerated computing platforms and AI solutions, networking, automotive platforms, Jetson for robotics, DGX Cloud. (FY2026 TTM: $193.5B)
2. **Graphics** -- GeForce GPUs for gaming, GeForce NOW, Quadro/RTX GPUs for workstations, vGPU software, automotive infotainment, Omniverse. (FY2026 TTM: $22.5B)
