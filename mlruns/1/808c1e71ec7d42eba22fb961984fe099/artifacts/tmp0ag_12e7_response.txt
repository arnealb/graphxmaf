To report a suspected phishing email and understand the consequences of failing a phishing test, follow these guidelines:

### Reporting a Suspected Phishing Email:
- **Immediate Action**: Report all suspected security incidents, including phishing attempts, as soon as possible.
- **Reporting Channels**:
  - **Security Portal**: Use [security.easi.net](http://security.easi.net).
  - **Email**: Send details to security@easi.net.
- **Important**: Do not investigate the incident on your own or attempt to clean the affected device; this could destroy evidence.
- **Assistance**: If you're unsure of the next steps, contact trained first responders Arne Albrecht or Dorian Feaux for help with reporting.

### Consequences of Failing a Phishing Test:
- **First Failure**: If you click a simulated phishing link or enter credentials in a phishing test:
  - You are required to complete a **30-minute online security awareness training**.
  - Your manager will be notified.
- **Second Failure** (within 12 months):
  - A discussion will be initiated with **HR** regarding your performance in phishing tests. 

By following these steps, you can effectively report phishing attempts and understand the implications of failing phishing tests.
